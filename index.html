Planet Pesto

UI & Level Designer | June 2025 ‚Äì July 2025

Project Overview

Planet Pesto is a 2D narrative-driven platformer developed for IndieCade's 2025 Climate Jam. The game explores climate change through the lens of a small robot named I.R.I.S., who discovers that humans are making Earth uninhabitable for themselves through pollution.

Achievement: 14th place out of 34 entries in IndieCade's 2025 Climate Jam competition
Play the game on itch.io ‚Üí

My Role

As part of a 10-person interdisciplinary team, I served as both UI Designer and Level Designer where I:

  • Created 4 UI assets in Adobe Illustrator with a cohesive steampunk-inspired aesthetic
  • Co-designed 4 platformer game levels featuring obstacles related to climate disasters
  • Led weekly level design team meetings to brainstorm, draft, and refine level designs
  • Incorporated peer and playtester feedback through iterative design sprints

Gameplay & Level Design

The game features a charming steampunk-inspired aesthetic set in various environments, from lush forests to industrial interiors. Players navigate through platforming challenges while learning about climate action.

Menu Design Process

For the game's pause menu, I initially created ten different menu concepts that each explored different menu layouts and styles. After receiving feedback from the rest of the team, I continued to iterate and make improvements to the design before ending up with the final version. Before starting my concept sketches, I had also conducted research and created a moodboard consisting of the pause menus of different games along with the general style I was aiming for to gain more inspiration that I later presented to the team. Going into the design process, the visual team knew that we wanted the menu to follow a steampunk aesthetic to complement the game's overall theme while maintaining clear usability.

Steam Gauge Process

Another key UI asset that I designed for Planet Pesto was the steam gauge which served as a resource meter in the game for the player's dash ability. Over ten concept versions, I explored many different visual approaches from circular gauges to horizontal bars while continuing to revise and rework following feedback from the design team.

Design System

The warm color palette of the menu and steam gauge was chosen to complement the game's steampunk aesthetic, creating visual cohesion between UI elements and the game world. For typography, I selected Berlin Sans FB for the pause menu text because of its excellent legibility, which is crucial for pause menus where players need to quickly read and make decisions.

Key Learnings

This project taught me the importance of iterative design in game development, the value of playtesting early and often, and how to balance artistic vision with usability constraints in an interactive medium. Leading weekly team meetings also strengthened my collaboration and communication skills.

Working on a climate-focused narrative game reinforced how design can communicate complex issues through interactive storytelling. The challenge of making environmental disasters both challenging gameplay obstacles and meaningful narrative moments pushed me to think critically about how mechanics support themes.

GreenWay

UX Researcher & Designer | February 2025 ‚Äì March 2025

Team: Grace Chau, Amelie Eng, Sofia Mraz, Ruoxi Wang

The Problem

Boston's MBTA Green Line commuters face numerous challenges including frequent delays, inconsistent train capacity, and difficulty navigating complex station layouts and surrounding areas. These pain points create stress and uncertainty for daily riders.

Research

I conducted user research to identify key commuter pain points which included:

  • Unpredictable delays without clear communication
  • Inconsistent train capacity leading to overcrowding
  • Navigation challenges within stations and surrounding areas
  • Lack of real-time service updates
  • Difficulty planning alternative routes during disruptions

The Solution

GreenWay is a mobile app designed to improve the Green Line commuter experience through real-time service updates, train capacity indicators, and enhanced wayfinding features including AR navigation within stations.

Key Features: Real-time delay notifications, train capacity visualization, station wayfinding, and route planning

Prototype Frames

Design Process

The design evolved through three major iteration cycles, each incorporating peer feedback and collaborative problem-solving. Early wireframes focused on information hierarchy, while later iterations refined the visual design and interaction patterns to ensure critical information was immediately accessible during stressful commute situations.

Design System

Color Palette: The green color scheme was chosen as a direct representation of the MBTA Green Line, creating immediate brand recognition and visual consistency with the transit system riders are already familiar with.

Typography: Inter was selected for its exceptional legibility and modern sans-serif aesthetic. The clean, geometric letterforms provide a sleek and contemporary look while ensuring text remains readable in high-stress commute situations.

Key Takeaways

This project reinforced the importance of designing for real-world constraints and user stress. Features needed to be immediately accessible and understandable, especially during high-pressure commute situations. The iterative process allowed us to refine the interface based on actual user needs rather than assumptions.

Sleep Survey

UX Researcher & Designer | March 2025 ‚Äì April 2025

Team: Grace Chau, Kayla Chen, Amelie Eng, Kaitlin Rattray

The Problem

College students and young professionals (ages 18-25) face two interconnected challenges: lack of mental clarity upon waking up and the need for dream reflection in today's stressful environment. Stress and emotions cause irregular sleep patterns that affect dreaming and cause grogginess, making it difficult to start the day productively.

Target Users: College students and post-grads with irregular sleep patterns or high stress levels who want to understand how daily stressors affect their sleep

Interaction Scenario

A Northeastern student is exhausted from studying for finals and hasn't gotten much sleep recently. She wakes up from a nightmare and opens Sleep Survey as part of her morning routine. She logs her emotions upon waking up and writes briefly about her nightmare. The app prompts her with questions, and while answering them, she feels herself fully waking up. After requesting the Sleep Survey API to give her an analysis of her dream she feels satisfied with her log and new understanding, she's now alert and ready to conquer her finals.

Research & Competitive Analysis

I analyzed existing dream apps to identify opportunities for innovation:

  • DreamApp: Uses algorithms and AI to analyze user input, provides dream symbol definitions
  • LucidScribe: Offers scientific tools for lucidity reactivation and dream incubation
  • Gap identified: No apps combined mental clarity enhancement with dream reflection specifically for stressed young adults

Core Features & Design Solution

Sleep Survey helps users achieve mental clarity through interactive morning reflection:

  • Morning Log: Rate sleep quality and emotions upon waking
  • Dream Categorization: Log dreams as one of five types (dream, nightmare, lucid dream, recurring dream, or no dream)
  • Interactive Questioning: Answer prompts about dreams to increase alertness and understanding
  • AI Analysis: Receive psychological insights and emotion tags (fear, uncertainty, relief, anxiety)
  • Stressor Tracking: Connect daily life events to dream content (e.g., a bad test grade can lead to a nightmare about the future)
  • Calendar Visualization: Color-coded monthly view to identify sleep patterns

The calming purple interface promotes relaxation while the structured prompts help users fully wake up and gain clarity.

Prototype Frames

Design System

Color Palette: The calming purple theme was deliberately chosen to promote relaxation and create a soothing environment conducive to reflection and rest. Purple evokes tranquility and introspection, aligning perfectly with the app's wellness focus.

Typography: Potta One was selected for titles and headings because visually evokes a calm, gentle feeling that complements the app's peaceful aesthetic. The rounded, friendly letterforms create an approachable and non-intimidating interface for users during vulnerable morning moments. Paired with the font Poor Story for body text, both work together to present a calming visual upon waking up.

Key Takeaways

Through working on Sleep Survey, I learned that designing for wellness and mental health required careful consideration of tone, timing, and the user's emotional state. Creating a safe, non-judgmental digital space also requires balancing functionality with emotional intelligence in every design decision.

Working in a collaborative team environment also reinforced my desire to always listen to diverse perspectives in ideation and the importance of iterative feedback in refining user experience.

Filosofia Type Specimen

Graphic Designer | September 2025 ‚Äì October 2025

Project Overview

This type specimen project showcases the Filosofia typeface through a series of three carefully designed postcards. Filosofia, created by Zuzana Licko, is a geometric interpretation of Bodoni that maintains optimum legibility while honoring historical letterpress aesthetics.

Design Approach

Each postcard explores a different aspect of the typeface:

  • Card 1: Font history and background
  • Card 2: Type anatomy and background on Filosofia Parma
  • Card 3: Glyph composition and Typeface specimen

The Maroon and Gold color scheme for the postcards were chosen to compliment the regal and intricate look of the Filosofia's glyphs.

Key Learnings

This project deepened my understanding of typography, layout design, and knowledge about the Filosofia typeface.

Life Influences Map

Information Designer | February 2025

Project Overview

This information design project visualizes my present-day life influences through the metaphor of computer folders and files. The design maps various aspects of my daily life using file sizes to represent importance and influence.

Design Concept

The project organizes life influences into eight main categories:

  • Taste in Food
  • Interests
  • Social Life
  • Culture
  • Personality
  • Entertainment
  • Academics
  • Fashion

Within each folder, individual files represent specific influences, with their size indicating the strength of their impact. Colored connections between files visualize different relationships and the different overlapping influences in my life.

Visual Design

Key Learnings

This project allowed me to gain experience in information design while also allowing me to self-reflect on what are the main factors that influence my life and how I can use this knowledge to grow as a person.
